Hi, I'm Sven Kiefer, an astrophysicist studying exoplanets.

Welcome to the fascinating field of exoplanets! I have studied exoplanets through observations during my Bachelor's and Master's and dived deep into the theory of planetary atmospheres during my PhD. Even after several years investigating these far-off worlds, I am still fascinated by their diversity, complexity, and beauty every day.

Current Research

I study the atmospheres of exoplanets. I am working with chemical kinetic networks, micro-physical cloud models, and global circulation models. My goal is to use detailed models to achieve precise predictions and connect them to observations. Check out my latest publications and research projects.

Teaching & Outreach

I'm taking active part in teaching by supervising lab sessions, bachelor theses and master theses. Outside of university, I organise and take part in outreach activities by giving talks and organising educational events.

Extra-Curricular Activities

With the head in the stars it is important to keep the feet on the ground. I find working on projects in diverse teams and chaotic environ­ments compelling – be it a game development team, the rocket team ARIS or the Massive Exoplanet MEME exhibtion.
